About Me

I am a M.A.Sc candidate in Electrical and Computer Engineering at York University, working under the supervision of Dr. Hina Tabassum. My research focuses on multi-user activity recognition from Wi-Fi Channel State Information (CSI), where I develop novel transformer-based architectures and self-supervised learning methods for efficient learning from wireless sensory data.

Research Interests

I am broadly interested in learning structure from sensory data at scale, which encompasses:

  • Representation Learning - Discovering meaningful representations from high-dimensional data
  • Self-Supervised Learning - Learning from unlabeled data without human supervision
  • World Modeling - Building predictive models of complex environments
  • Generative Modeling - Inference and synthesis from sensory data

My work bridges the gap between theoretical machine learning and practical applications in wireless sensing, with a focus on developing efficient, scalable solutions for edge devices.
